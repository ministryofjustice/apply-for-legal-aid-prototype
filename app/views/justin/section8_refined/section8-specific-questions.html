Section 8 specific questions

We identified a number of questions relating specifically to Section 8 that we may ask you when making an application (to assist our caseworkers in making the correct decision).

Could you tell us what are your thoughts about those questions, and whether you would have the information to answer them at the point of application?

Question 1

If you select a Specific issue proceeding

  • what specific issue do you want to determine at court?

Question 2

If you select a Prohibited steps proceeding

  • What are you asking to be prohibited?
  • Is it to prevent removal from the UK?

Question 3

If you have used delegated functions because the matter is urgent

  • What is the nature of the urgency?
  • What specific work needs to be done as a matter of urgency?
  • Has a hearing been listed?

Question 4

If the role of your client (client involvement type) is ‘Defendant’

  • Upload supporting evidence - would it be in the form of a copy of the application made to the court?

Question 5

If you are applying to vary an existing court order

  • Why are the Court likely to agree to vary the order (having consideration to the length of time since the making of the original order)?

Question 6

A general question for all Section 8 proceedings

  • Why is the matter opposed?

And finally...

Are there any other questions that you are asked in Requests for information (after submitting an application) from the LAA Caseworkers?
