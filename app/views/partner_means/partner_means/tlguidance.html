Share bank statements with online banking


The partner can share their bank statements with us through online banking through a third party called TrueLayer.

It's a fast, safe and accurate way to access the partner's transactions and means the partner does not need to gather statements.


What is TrueLayer?

TrueLayer is a trusted third-party financial service provider that acts as a messenger between the partner's bank and the Legal Aid Agency (LAA).

TrueLayer needs one-time, read-only access to the partner's bank account information. If the partner agrees, TrueLayer will securely collect 3 months of statements and share them with the LAA.

They're regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and will not:

  • store the partner's bank account information
  • see the partner's online banking sign-in details
  • share the partner's data with anyone other than the LAA
  • have ongoing access to the partner's bank accounts

How it works


1. Get started

The partner will receive an email clearly labelled from the Legal Aid Agency (LAA) asking them to share 3 months of bank statements.

There's a link in the email directing them to 'Apply for legal aid' to get started.

2. Select a bank

The partner selects their bank then signs in to their online banking website or app.

3. Share bank accounts

The partner must share all of their bank accounts, including joint accounts and accounts with no money in them. Bank statements from the last 3 months will be shared securely with the LAA.


4. Confirmation

The partner will see a message confirming they’ve successfully shared their bank statements. They’ll be asked if they have any more bank accounts to share.

5. What happens next

You need to sort the partner’s transactions into categories so we can understand their financial situation and process the application quickly.
