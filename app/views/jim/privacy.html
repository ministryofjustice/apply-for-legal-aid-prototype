Privacy policy

What data we collect

The data we collect from you includes:

  • personal details like your name, address, date of birth, contact details and National Insurance number
  • financial information to check if you qualify for legal aid
  • your Internet Protocol (IP) address, and details of which version of web browser you used
  • information on how you use the site, using cookies and page tagging techniques
  • any feedback you leave

We use Google Analytics software to collect information about how you use the service.

Google Analytics stores information about:

  • the pages you visit
  • how long you spend on each page
  • how you got to the service
  • what you click on while you’re using the service

We do not collect or store your personal information through Google Analytics (for example your name or address) so this information cannot be used to identify who you are.

Why we need your data

We collect data to:

  • work out whether you qualify for legal aid
  • make sure the service meets the needs of its users
  • gather statistics about the service
  • help us monitor and improve the service
  • prevent or detect crime or fraud
  • justify public expenditure
  • carry out audits to ensure that the decisions we make are correct and accurate

The legal basis for processing your personal data is the result of the powers contained in the Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012.

What we do with your data

We’ll share your personal data with your solicitor so they can complete your legal aid application.

We may also share your data with:

  • other government departments, agencies and public bodies
  • non-public organisations such as credit reference and debt collection agencies
  • our technology suppliers, for example our hosting provider

We will not:

  • sell or rent your data to third parties
  • share your data with third parties for marketing purposes

We will share your data if the law requires us to – for example, by court order, or to prevent fraud or other crime.

Where your data is processed and stored

We design, build and run our systems to make sure that your data is as safe as possible at all stages, both while it’s processed and when it’s stored.

All personal data is stored in the European Economic Area (EEA). Google Analytics data may be transferred outside the EEA, but this cannot be used to personally identify you.

How we protect your data and keep it secure

We are committed to doing all that we can to keep your data secure. We have set up systems and processes to prevent unauthorised access or disclosure of your data – for example, we protect your data using varying levels of encryption.

We also make sure that any third parties that we deal with keep all personal data they process on our behalf secure.

How long we keep your data

We will only retain your personal data for as long as:

  • it is needed for the purposes set out in this document
  • the law requires us to

View our policy on retaining data, or request a copy from our Data Protection Officer.

Your rights

You have the right to request:

  • information about how your personal data is processed
  • a copy of that personal data (a 'subject access request')
  • that anything inaccurate in your personal data is corrected
  • that your personal data is erased if there is no longer a justification for it
  • a copy of any data-sharing agreements we have with other organisations
  • information on how we instruct staff to collect, use or delete your personal data

If you have any of these requests, contact the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) Data Protection Officer.
