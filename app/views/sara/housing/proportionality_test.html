What is the proportionality test?
The proportionality test checks if the costs of a case are fair compared to the value it brings. It is judged by:

  • how much money is at stake
  • value of non-monetary outcomes
  • wider factors, like public importance or reputation

The test is met if:

  • for damage claims, the ratio of damages to costs is reasonable
  • for non-damage claims, the costs are fair in relation to how important or significant the outcome is

If the case is not cost-effective, it considers:

  • the seriousness of the issues raised if they are significant enough to justify the costs
  • if someone would be willing or able to pay for the case themselves
